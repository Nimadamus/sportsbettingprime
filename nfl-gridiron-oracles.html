NFC Wild Card: 49ers vs Eagles

Here's the thing about this game - the line movement tells you everything. This opened at Eagles -3 and has climbed all the way to -5.5. That's not public money. That's sharp action hammering Philly, and I get why. The Eagles are defending Super Bowl champions for a reason, and that reason is they're built perfectly for January football.

San Francisco comes in at 12-5, but don't let that record fool you. They're 1-7 ATS in their last eight games as underdogs. That's not a coincidence - it's a trend that matters. Meanwhile, 65% of the bets are on the Niners to cover, but 57% of the actual money is on the Eagles. When the public goes one way and the sharps go another, you follow the sharps.

Look, Lincoln Financial Field in January is a different beast. The cold weather, the hostile crowd, the playoff intensity - Philly thrives in this environment. The total dropped from 46.5 to 44.5, which tells me both sides expect a defensive battle. In games like this, home field and coaching matter more than raw talent. The Eagles have both.

AFC Wild Card: Chargers vs Patriots

Drake Maye might be the MVP of this league, and tonight he gets his first playoff start at Gillette Stadium. This is a fascinating matchup because Jim Harbaugh's Chargers are built to win these types of road games - they've gone 4-1 ATS as underdogs this season, including 3-1 on the road. That's not a team that folds under pressure.

But here's what concerns me about LA. Justin Herbert is banged up, and they're missing their top two offensive tackles. In January football at Gillette, that's a recipe for disaster. The cold weather slows down that West Coast passing attack, and the Patriots' defense is built to make you uncomfortable. New England has covered -3.5 in 8 of their last 10 games. That's dominance.

The market opened this at Patriots -4 and it's ticked down to -3.5. That tells me there's some Chargers money coming in, which makes sense given Harbaugh's pedigree. But this is the Patriots' first playoff home game since 2020, and Gillette is going to be rocking. In primetime, at home, with their season on the line? I trust Maye and this Patriots squad to get it done.
