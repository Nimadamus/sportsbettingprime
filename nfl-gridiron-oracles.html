AFC Championship: Patriots vs Broncos Deep Dive

The Bo Nix Effect: This line tells you everything you need to know about how devastating the Nix injury is for Denver. Before the broken ankle, look-ahead lines had the Broncos as 1.5-point home favorites. Now? The Patriots are road favorites by nearly a touchdown. That's a 7-point swing entirely due to one player going down.

Jarrett Stidham takes over for Denver, and this is where things get interesting. Stidham has NFL experience, having started games for the Raiders in 2023, but he's 2-6 as a career starter with a 12:8 TD:INT ratio. He's a game manager at best, which means Sean Payton will need to lean heavily on the running game and defense to keep this competitive.

The Patriots' Path: New England has been the most complete team in the AFC this postseason. Their 28-16 dismantling of Houston showcased a defense that can take away what opponents do best. The Patriots held C.J. Stroud to under 200 passing yards and picked him off twice. If that's what they do to a healthy starting QB, imagine what they'll do to Stidham.

Weather Factor: January in Denver means altitude and potential cold. The thin air at Mile High typically helps offenses, but with Stidham's limited arm talent and the Patriots' ball-control style, this could become a rock fight. The under of 40.5 looks sharp here.

Sharp Money Watch: Early money pounded New England from -4.5 to -6 at some shops before settling at the consensus -5.5. The under has also moved from 42 to 40.5. Professional bettors clearly see this as a low-scoring affair with New England controlling the tempo.

NFC Championship: Rams vs Seahawks Deep Dive

The Third Meeting: These NFC West rivals know each other intimately, having split their regular season series with the road team winning both times by razor-thin margins. That trend is fascinating heading into Seattle, where the Seahawks have been nearly unbeatable at home this season (7-2). Does the road warrior trend continue, or does home field finally assert itself in the biggest game of the year?

Geno Smith's Redemption Arc: The Seahawks QB has been sensational in the playoffs. His 4-touchdown demolition of San Francisco wasn't just impressive, it was dominant. After years of being written off as a journeyman, Smith has Seattle one win away from the Super Bowl. The 41-6 victory over the 49ers was a statement: this team is for real.

Matthew Stafford's Quest: The Rams QB is no stranger to playoff pressure, having won Super Bowl LVI. But Stafford's hand injury, which he gutted through against Chicago in snowy conditions, remains a storyline. He was clearly limited in the OT win and will need to be sharper against Seattle's aggressive defense. Harrison Mevis's 42-yard game-winner kept the dream alive, but the Rams can't rely on overtime heroics again.

The Spread Analysis: Seattle -2.5 essentially makes this a pick'em after home field. The line has crept up to -3 at some books with juice on LA at +3 -120. The market respects the Rams' playoff experience and Stafford's championship pedigree, but also recognizes Seattle's dominance at home.

Super Bowl Preview: Seattle (+145) is the betting favorite to win it all, with the Rams at +210. This projects to be the higher-scoring, more entertaining game of Championship Sunday. Both offenses are capable of explosive plays, and the total of 47.5 reflects that expectation.
