Houston Rockets at Indiana Pacers

The Pacers return home after a disappointing loss to Kevin Durant and the Rockets just three weeks ago. Indiana's pace-and-space offense is built for Gainbridge Fieldhouse, where they average 122.4 points per game this season, second-best in the league at home.

Houston comes in riding high after their Western Conference surge, but road games in Indiana have been problematic for visitors. The Pacers are 18-8 at home, and their tempo could be the difference-maker here. Both teams push pace, which explains the elevated 236.5 total.

The key matchup is the backcourt battle. Tyrese Haliburton's playmaking versus Fred VanVleet's absence due to ACL injury tilts this toward Indiana. The Rockets will rely heavily on Jalen Green and Alperen Sengun to generate offense against a Pacers defense that has improved significantly since mid-January.

New Orleans Pelicans at Charlotte Hornets

A battle between two teams searching for answers in disappointing seasons. The Pelicans have been decimated by injuries all year, with Brandon Ingram traded to Toronto and Zion Williamson missing extended time. Charlotte, meanwhile, has struggled to find consistency with LaMelo Ball carrying an enormous offensive burden.

New Orleans being favored on the road against Charlotte reflects just how poorly the Hornets have played at home, where they're 8-18 at Spectrum Center. The Pelicans have shown flashes of competitiveness in recent weeks, winning three of their last seven games.

This is a game where the under deserves attention. Both teams rank bottom-10 in offensive efficiency, and the pace will likely be slower than expected as neither squad has the firepower for a shootout. The 218.5 total is on the lower end, but these teams have combined for under 215 points in three of their last five matchups.

Philadelphia 76ers at Los Angeles Clippers

The Sixers head West in a brutal stretch of their schedule, facing the Clippers in their new Intuit Dome. Philadelphia's season has been a disappointment, with Joel Embiid's recurring health issues and the supporting cast failing to pick up the slack. The Clippers, meanwhile, are a transformed team post-Kawhi and Paul George era, building around younger pieces.

The Clippers are a different beast at Intuit Dome, where they're 16-8 this season. Their home court advantage is real, enhanced by one of the best fan atmospheres in the league at their new arena. The 76ers are just 9-16 on the road, struggling to generate consistent offense away from Wells Fargo Center.

Philadelphia's fatigue is a factor here. This is the second game of a road trip, and the late 10:30 PM ET start means their bodies are still on East Coast time. The Clippers should control tempo and exploit Philly's transition defense.

San Antonio Spurs at Memphis Grizzlies

The rising Spurs, now led by De'Aaron Fox after his trade from Sacramento, visit a Memphis team that has been one of the hottest in the Western Conference. Ja Morant and the Grizzlies are 18-5 since December 1st, making FedExForum one of the toughest buildings in the league.

Fox's arrival has transformed San Antonio from a rebuilding project into a legitimate playoff contender. Paired with Victor Wembanyama, the Spurs now have an elite backcourt-frontcourt duo that can compete with anyone. But road games against the Grizzlies' physical style remain a challenge.

Memphis's home court advantage is well-documented. They're 17-6 at FedExForum, and their defensive intensity at home is suffocating. The 6.5-point spread reflects the market's confidence in the Grizzlies, though Fox gives San Antonio a legitimate chance to keep this close.
