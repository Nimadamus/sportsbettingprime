The Matt Entz Paradigm Shift

To understand Entz is to understand his philosophy: dominate the line of scrimmage, run the football, and bleed the clock. This isn't speculation; it's a proven model. At North Dakota State, his teams were a ground-and-pound machine, running the ball on over 70% of their offensive snaps.

He’s not coming to Fresno to air it out. He's here to instill a culture of toughness and physicality. What does that mean for this game?

  • Fewer Possessions: A run-heavy, clock-controlling offense shortens the game for both teams. Fewer possessions mean fewer opportunities to score.
  • Defensive Identity: Entz is a defensive coach at his core. Expect Fresno State to play fundamentally sound, disciplined football, aiming to win a 24-20 slugfest, not a 35-31 shootout.

Offensive Disruption vs. Defensive Strength

Both offenses are entering Week 0 with significant question marks that play directly into the under.

Kansas, despite the talent of QB Jalon Daniels, is replacing its top wide receivers and its all-time leading rusher. Building chemistry and timing with a new cast of weapons doesn't happen overnight, especially against a formidable opponent.

And that opponent is Fresno State's strength: its secondary. The Bulldogs boasted a top-12 pass defense last season and return multiple key starters. This isn't the soft matchup an offense needs to find its rhythm in a season opener.

On the other side, Fresno State is breaking in a new quarterback in E.J. Warner, who is also losing his top three receiving targets from last year. Under a new, run-first coach, the game plan will be to protect the new QB and lean on the defense, not ask him to win the game with his arm.
