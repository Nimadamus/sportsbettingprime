College football takes a well-deserved break on December 25th. Bowl season resumes tomorrow with three exciting matchups. Check out our December 26th previews below, plus yesterday's Hawaii Bowl result.

December 26 Bowl Previews

GameAbove Sports Bowl December 26 | 12:00 PM ET | Ford Field
Central Michigan
6-6
VS
Northwestern
5-7

Here's an interesting MAC vs Big Ten matchup to kick off the day after Christmas. Central Michigan earned their bowl berth the hard way, finishing .500 in a competitive Mid-American Conference. Northwestern, meanwhile, snuck in at 5-7 with an Academic Progress Rate exemption - not exactly a ringing endorsement.

The Chippewas have the motivation edge here. Playing in Detroit at Ford Field is essentially a home game for Central Michigan, with their campus just about two hours north. The Wildcats have struggled offensively all season, ranking near the bottom of the Big Ten in scoring. I think CMU's familiarity with the venue and their chip-on-the-shoulder mentality as a Group of Five team facing a Power Four opponent makes them live underdogs in this one.

Rate Bowl December 26 | 3:30 PM ET | Chase Field
New Mexico
6-6
VS
Minnesota
7-5

Don't sleep on New Mexico. The Lobos finished their regular season strong and have a legitimate passing attack that can hang with anyone. Minnesota comes in as the favorite, but the Golden Gophers lost three of their last four games to close out the regular season - not exactly the kind of momentum you want heading into bowl season.

This game comes down to whether Minnesota can establish their ground game. The Gophers are built to run the ball and control the clock, but New Mexico's defense has been feisty against the run all year. If the Lobos can force Minnesota into obvious passing situations, their pass rush could wreak havoc on a Gophers offensive line that's given up too many sacks. The desert setting in Phoenix at Chase Field also neutralizes any weather advantage for the Big Ten squad. This spread feels like it's asking too much from Minnesota.

First Responder Bowl December 26 | 7:00 PM ET | Gerald J. Ford Stadium
FIU
7-5
VS
UTSA
6-6

Two Conference USA programs duke it out in Dallas in what should be a fun, under-the-radar matchup. FIU has been one of the better stories in the Group of Five this season, putting together a 7-5 record that exceeded most preseason expectations. UTSA, the perennial C-USA power, had a more turbulent year but still found their way to bowl eligibility.

The Panthers come in with better overall numbers, but UTSA has the experience edge. The Roadrunners have made bowl games in each of the last four seasons and know how to prepare for these postseason matchups. That said, FIU's defense has been stingy, ranking in the top 30 nationally in scoring defense. If they can force UTSA's offense into mistakes, the Panthers have the firepower to capitalize. I expect a close, competitive game that comes down to the fourth quarter.

FINAL Hawaii Bowl - December 24, 2025
Hawaii
35
FINAL
California
31

What a way to cap off Christmas Eve! Hawaii pulled off the upset at home, defeating ACC foe California 35-31 in an absolute thriller. The Rainbow Warriors came into this game as 3-point underdogs and proved why you should never count out a team playing in their home stadium during bowl season.

Brayden Schager was masterful for Hawaii, orchestrating a balanced attack that kept the Cal defense guessing all night. The Warriors converted key third downs in the fourth quarter and the home crowd at Clarence T.C. Ching Athletics Complex was absolutely electric. For California, it's a disappointing end to their first ACC season after joining from the Pac-12. They showed flashes but couldn't close it out when it mattered most.
