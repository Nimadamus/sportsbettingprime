What is Covers Consensus?

Covers Consensus aggregates betting picks from the top 200 handicappers on Covers.com, ranked by units won in their contests. When multiple experts agree on a pick, it creates a "consensus" - and the higher the consensus, the more experts are aligned on that bet.

Here's how to read the consensus data:

  • High Consensus (15+ experts): Strong agreement among top handicappers. These picks have historically shown value.
  • Medium Consensus (8-14 experts): Moderate agreement. Worth considering alongside your own analysis.
  • Low Consensus (2-7 experts): Split opinions. These picks may offer contrarian value.

We update this page daily before games start, pulling the latest picks from Covers.com contest leaderboards. Each day's consensus is archived so you can track historical performance.

Frequently Asked Questions About Covers Consensus

What is Covers Consensus?
Covers Consensus shows what the top handicappers on Covers.com are betting. We track the top 200 contestants ranked by units won and aggregate their picks to show which bets have the highest expert agreement. When 20+ experts agree on a pick, it indicates strong consensus.
How often are consensus picks updated?
Consensus picks are updated daily, typically in the morning before games begin. Each day's picks are archived so you can track historical performance and see how consensus picks have performed over time.
What sports are covered in the consensus picks?
We track consensus picks for NFL, NBA, NHL, and college basketball (NCAAB). During football season, we also cover college football (NCAAF). Each sport has its own dedicated consensus page for focused analysis.
How should I use consensus picks in my betting strategy?
Consensus picks work best as one input among many in your betting strategy. High consensus (15+ experts) suggests strong agreement, but you should always do your own research. Many bettors use consensus as confirmation of their own analysis or as a starting point for further research.
What does the consensus number mean?
The consensus number shows how many of the top 200 Covers.com handicappers agree on a particular pick. For example, "28 Experts" means 28 of the top 200 handicappers have this pick. Higher numbers indicate stronger agreement among successful bettors.
Where does the consensus data come from?
Our consensus data comes directly from Covers.com contest leaderboards. We track the top 200 contestants ranked by units won and aggregate their publicly visible picks. This ensures you're seeing picks from proven, successful handicappers.
