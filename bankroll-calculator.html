Understanding the Kelly Criterion

The Kelly Criterion is a mathematical formula that determines the optimal bet size to maximize long-term growth while minimizing the risk of ruin. It was developed by John Kelly at Bell Labs in 1956.

Kelly % = (bp - q) / b

b = decimal odds - 1 | p = win probability | q = loss probability (1 - p)

Why Kelly Matters

Betting too much of your bankroll on any single bet exposes you to unnecessary risk. Betting too little leaves money on the table. Kelly finds the sweet spot - the exact amount that maximizes your expected growth rate.

Pro Tip: Use Fractional Kelly

Most professional bettors use "half Kelly" or "quarter Kelly" - betting 25-50% of what the Kelly formula suggests. This reduces volatility while still capturing most of the expected growth. Full Kelly can lead to large drawdowns that are psychologically difficult to handle.

When Kelly Doesn't Apply

The Kelly Criterion assumes you know your true edge - but in sports betting, this is nearly impossible to calculate precisely. Use Kelly as a guide, not gospel. If you're unsure about your edge, flat betting (same amount on every bet) is a safer approach.

Critical Warning: Edge Estimation

If you overestimate your edge, Kelly will tell you to bet too much. If you think you have a 55% win rate but you actually have 50%, Kelly could lead you to ruin. Always be conservative in your edge estimates.

Bankroll Management Principles

The Golden Rules

Professional sports bettors follow strict bankroll management rules. These aren't suggestions - they're the difference between long-term success and going broke.

  • Never bet more than 5% on a single game - Even your strongest plays should be capped
  • Don't chase losses - Increasing bet sizes after losses is a path to ruin
  • Track every bet - You can't improve what you don't measure
  • Reassess unit size monthly - As your bankroll grows or shrinks, adjust
  • Separate gambling money from living expenses - Only bet what you can afford to lose

The 1-3-5 Unit System

Many handicappers use a tiered confidence system:

  • 1 Unit: Standard plays - slight edge identified
  • 3 Units: Strong plays - clear edge with multiple supporting factors
  • 5 Units: Max plays - only 1-2 per month, very high confidence

Variance is Real

Even a 55% bettor will experience 10+ game losing streaks. Your bankroll needs to survive these inevitable downswings. At 2% units, a 10-game losing streak only costs 20% of your bankroll - painful but survivable. At 10% units, you're down 65% and likely out of the game.
