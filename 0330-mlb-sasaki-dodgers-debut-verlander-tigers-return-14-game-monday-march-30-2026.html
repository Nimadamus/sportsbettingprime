MLB Monday

Roki Sasaki's Dodgers Debut, Verlander Returns to Detroit, and a Massive 14-Game Monday Slate

March 30, 2026 · 8 min read · Sports Betting Prime

Roki Sasaki takes the mound for the Dodgers for the first time in his career on Monday night | Photo: MLB.com

Monday's 14-game MLB slate is an absolute circus of storylines, and we haven't even finished the first full week of the season yet. Roki Sasaki, the most hyped international signing of the decade, makes his long-awaited Dodgers debut against Cleveland. Justin Verlander, 43 years old and apparently immortal, returns to Detroit on a one-year deal and faces Arizona. Ranger Suarez pitches in a Red Sox uniform for the first time after his $130 million move from Philadelphia. Walker Buehler debuts for the Padres against his former Dodger teammates' division rival. And five teams are still undefeated while five others are winless and already searching for answers. Let's break down every major storyline, game by game, with full betting lines.

Roki Sasaki's MLB Debut: Guardians at Dodgers, 10:10 PM ET

Cleveland Guardians (2-2) at Los Angeles Dodgers (3-0)
Parker Messick vs Roki Sasaki | CLE +163 / LAD -199 | RL: CLE +1.5 (-126) / LAD -1.5 (+105) | O/U 9

This is the game the entire baseball world has been waiting for. Roki Sasaki, the 24-year-old Japanese phenom who threw a perfect game in NPB and was the most coveted free agent outside of the major league system, finally takes the mound as a Dodger. The hype is real, but the spring training numbers suggest you should temper expectations for Game 1. Sasaki allowed 15 earned runs and walked 15 batters in just 8.2 innings this spring, striking out 12. The command was all over the place. The Dodgers have Andrew Wrobleski on standby if things go sideways early, which tells you everything about their comfort level.

But here's the thing, this is still the Dodgers chasing a historic three-peat. No team has won three straight World Series titles since the 1998-2000 Yankees, and Los Angeles looks absolutely loaded again. Kyle Tucker, acquired from the Astros, slots right into this lineup and makes an already terrifying order even deeper. The Dodgers opened at 3-0 and haven't shown any signs of slowing down.

Cleveland brings its own storyline. Chase DeLauter has four home runs in his first three career games, the most by any player since Trevor Story's incredible debut in 2016. The kid is a menace right now, and if Sasaki's command issues from spring carry over, DeLauter could have a field day. Parker Messick gets the ball for the Guardians in a tough spot, but that -199 Dodgers moneyline tells you the market is betting on the moment more than the matchup. The 9-run total is notable, suggesting oddsmakers expect runs whether Sasaki's shaky spring was a fluke or not.

Verlander's Homecoming: Tigers at Diamondbacks, 10:10 PM ET

Detroit Tigers (2-1) at Arizona Diamondbacks (0-3)
Justin Verlander vs Michael Soroka | DET +104 / ARI -126 | RL: DET -1.5 (+155) / ARI +1.5 (-188) | O/U 9

Justin Verlander is a Tiger again. Let that sink in. The future Hall of Famer signed a one-year, $13 million deal to come back to the franchise where he became a legend, and Monday marks his first start in a Detroit uniform since 2017. That was the year he was traded to the Astros at the deadline, and the rest is history: two more Cy Young awards, a World Series ring, and 2,000-plus more strikeouts. Now he's 43, and he's home.

The matchup is fascinating from a market perspective. Arizona is 0-3 and looks lost. They're the shortest home favorite on the board at -126, which is essentially the market saying, "We don't really trust either of these teams right now." Michael Soroka is still working his way back to the pitcher he was before the Achilles injuries, and Arizona's bats have been dormant through the first series. Detroit at 2-1 has some early-season momentum, and the emotional lift of Verlander's return could make this a dangerous spot for a reeling Diamondbacks team. The 9-run total is interesting given that neither of these pitchers inspires a ton of confidence in terms of going deep into games.

The Aaron Judge Show: Yankees at Mariners, 9:40 PM ET

New York Yankees (3-0) at Seattle Mariners (2-2)
Ryan Weathers vs Luis Castillo | NYY -122 / SEA +102 | RL: NYY -1.5 (+139) / SEA +1.5 (-168) | O/U 7.5

The Yankees are one of five undefeated teams through the first week, and the way they've been winning is absolutely suffocating. New York swept San Francisco while allowing just one run across three games. That's the kind of pitching depth that wins in October, and we're seeing it in March. Aaron Judge looks locked in already, and the intrigue here is the matchup with Cal Raleigh. These two were the AL MVP rivals in 2025, and now they're on the same field again.

Luis Castillo has the stuff to shut down any lineup in the sport, and at home, Seattle is a tough place to hit. The 7.5 total is the second-lowest on the board, reflecting both the pitching matchup and T-Mobile Park's suppressive environment. Ryan Weathers gets the ball for New York, and while he doesn't have Castillo's pedigree, the Yankees' lineup can carry a game regardless of who's on the mound. That -122 moneyline makes the Yankees a light road favorite, essentially a pick-em game with a slight lean toward the pinstripes. This feels like a low-scoring, tightly contested affair that could come down to one big swing.

Ranger Suarez in Red Sox Debut: Boston at Houston, 8:10 PM ET

Boston Red Sox (1-2) at Houston Astros (2-2)
Ranger Suarez vs Lance McCullers Jr | BOS -126 / HOU +104 | RL: BOS -1.5 (+129) / HOU +1.5 (-156) | O/U 8.5

The Red Sox made the biggest pitching investment of the offseason when they signed Ranger Suarez to a five-year, $130 million contract from the Phillies, and tonight is the payoff. Suarez was one of the most consistent starters in baseball over the last three-plus seasons, posting a 3.59 ERA over 104 starts from 2022 through 2025. He throws strikes, limits damage, and gives you innings. That's exactly what Boston needed after years of rotation instability.

Houston counters with Lance McCullers Jr., who continues to battle back from the injuries that have defined the second half of his career. The Astros are 2-2 and in a very different place than the dynasty years, particularly after losing Kyle Tucker to the Dodgers. That said, Minute Maid Park is still a tough place for visiting teams, and McCullers at his best has the arsenal to compete with anyone. The 8.5 total and Boston's -126 line suggest the market views this as Suarez's game to control, with the Red Sox getting the nod primarily on the strength of their new ace.

Walker Buehler's Fresh Start: Giants at Padres, 9:40 PM ET (FS1)

San Francisco Giants (0-3) at San Diego Padres (1-2)
Landen Roupp vs Walker Buehler | SF -102 / SD -118 | RL: SF -1.5 (+150) / SD +1.5 (-182) | O/U 8.5

Walker Buehler left the Dodgers this offseason and landed in San Diego looking for a fresh start. The former World Series hero dealt with elbow problems for years, had Tommy John surgery, and struggled through a rough 2024 regular season before flipping a switch in the postseason. Now he says he's pain-free for the first time since before his 2022 Tommy John surgery, and that alone makes this start fascinating to watch.

The Giants come in winless at 0-3 and desperately need a jolt. Landen Roupp gets the start against a Padres team that's been uneven itself at 1-2. The moneyline is essentially a coin flip, with San Diego getting a slight -118 nod as the home team. The 8.5 total implies both offenses should be able to score, which makes sense given the question marks on the mound. If Buehler's health is truly behind him, this Padres rotation just got significantly more dangerous. If the elbow is still a concern, we'll see it early. National TV game on FS1 adds a spotlight to what should be a compelling NL West showdown between two struggling teams looking to right the ship.

Bregman and the Cubs Look to Get Going: Angels at Cubs, 7:40 PM ET

LA Angels (2-2) at Chicago Cubs (1-2)
Ryan Johnson vs Edward Cabrera | LAA +159 / CHC -194 | O/U 10

The highest total on Monday's board sits right here at Wrigley Field. The 10-run number reflects the pitching matchup: Ryan Johnson for the Angels against Edward Cabrera for the Cubs. Neither inspires a ton of confidence, and the market is expecting fireworks. Alex Bregman and the Cubs opened the season 1-2, which obviously isn't the start Chicago was hoping for after spending big money to bring the former Astros star to the North Side. Bregman's bat will wake up eventually, and a soft matchup against the Angels' pitching might be just what the doctor ordered.

The Angels are 2-2 and have been competitive, but they're sizable underdogs at +159 on the road. That's a lot of juice on a Cubs team that hasn't proven anything yet this season. Chicago's -194 moneyline says the market thinks the talent gap is significant, but early-season lines in baseball can be deceiving. If Cabrera doesn't have his best stuff, the Angels have the kind of lineup that can hang around and make this interesting. Either way, the 10-run total screams "sit back and watch the chaos."

The Undefeated and the Winless

  • Still Perfect (3-0 or better): Dodgers, Blue Jays, Yankees, Brewers, Marlins. Five teams haven't lost yet, and three of them (LAD, NYY, TOR) are among the biggest favorites on Monday's board.
  • Still Searching (0-3): Diamondbacks, Rockies, Giants, White Sox, Athletics. Five winless clubs, all of them playing tonight. Arizona's 0-3 start with home games is the most alarming given their preseason expectations.
  • Debut Watch: Sasaki (LAD), Verlander homecoming (DET), Suarez (BOS), and Buehler (SD) all making significant first appearances. Four games with massive narrative weight.
  • Highest Total: Angels/Cubs O/U 10.0. Lowest Total: Yankees/Mariners O/U 7.5. That 2.5-run gap tells you everything about how the market views these pitching matchups.
  • DeLauter Watch: Cleveland's Chase DeLauter has 4 HR in 3 career games, the most since Trevor Story's iconic debut in 2016. If Sasaki can't locate tonight, look out.

Monday night is the kind of slate that reminds you why baseball season is the best stretch on the sports calendar. Fourteen games, half a dozen debut storylines, five teams trying to stay perfect, and five more trying to avoid an 0-4 hole that can set the tone for weeks. Sasaki's debut in Dodger Stadium is the headliner, but don't sleep on Verlander's emotional return to Detroit or the value lurking in some of these underdog spots. It's going to be a long, loud, glorious night of baseball.
